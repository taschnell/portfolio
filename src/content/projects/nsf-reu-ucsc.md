## Overview

Selected to participate in an NSF Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) hosted by UCSC’s [Materials Research Laboratory](https://materials.ucsc.edu/), working in the [Radiological Instrumentation Laboratory](https://ril.soe.ucsc.edu/) under the mentorship of Professor Shiva Abbaszadeh.

The project focuses on alloyed amorphous chalcogenide semiconductor materials for high-performance UV and X-ray photodetectors.

## Research Focus

Research investigates how modifications to amorphous selenium and related materials can improve detector performance, including:

- UV and X-ray detection sensitivity
- Charge carrier transport properties
- Device stability and reliability
- Photodetector response behavior

## Planned Activities

- Semiconductor device fabrication
- Thin-film deposition techniques
- Electrical and optical characterization
- Data analysis and visualization
- Scientific presentations and reporting
